Our Mission

At VietSight, we believe in promoting the local Vietnamese enconomy through a community of people enthusiastic about sharing and experiencing the treasure trove of hidden gems Vietnam has to offer.

Imagine this. You arrive in a new city, perhaps for travel or maybe you are relocating, and you have no idea what to do.

Where should you eat? Where should you grab a drink? What cool places are there to visit?

You might decide that in order to find answers to these questions, you should look up some travel blogs and see what they recommend.

The famous Bitexco Tower in Ho Chi Minh.

It's not a bad idea, but there is a problem. All the travel blogs tend to recommend the same list of places. Most bloggers use other blogs to find places to go and tend to have the same experiences. It takes a lot of effort to find the 1 or 2 websites really offering anything different.

It's a huge shame. Vietnam boasts thousands of little known local businesses, but oftentimes these hidden gems tend to be so much better and unique.

Now more than ever, local businesses are in need of support as they struggle to bounce back from the devastating impact of Covid-19.

That's where VietSight comes in.

A local Vietnamese business.

VietSight is a social media platform aiming to share and promote local businesses and hidden places in Vietnam.

Been to a really great restaurant recently which served food you had never heard of? Let other people know about it!

Found a secret spot to watch the sunset over the Saigon River? Share it!

Stumble upon a really great place for photographers to snap an image of the renowned Dragon Bridge of Danang? Help the community out!

One of many street sellers with delicious fruits for sale.

At VietSight, we believe in promoting the cultural diversity of Vietnam, it's wide palette of curious dishes (not just rice and noodles!) and it's bountiful beauty.

Setting up an account is easy and only takes a minute. Once in, you can share your experiences with the community of expats and locals in Vietnam, and save posts you like to your own bucket list.

Next time you are unsure what to do in your free time, check out your bucket list for inspiration.

Support the local community and explore a side of Vietnam which the travel blogs don't show you!
